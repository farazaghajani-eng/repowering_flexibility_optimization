# Research Trend Document: European Union Research Trends in Power Systems Flexibility  

## Introduction  
This document explores the current research trends in the European Union regarding power systems flexibility, generation planning, renewable integration, and optimization. 

## Top-tier Universities  
1. **ETH Zurich (Switzerland)**  
   - Notable Research Group: Power Systems and Energy Economics  
   - Research Focus: Generation planning, optimization methods for renewable energy sources.
 
2. **Imperial College London (UK)**  
   - Notable Research Group: Centre for Energy Policy  
   - Research Focus: Flexible energy systems, energy storage integration.
 
3. **Delft University of Technology (Netherlands)**  
   - Notable Research Group: Electrical Sustainable Energy  
   - Research Focus: Optimization under uncertainty in renewable energy systems.
 
## Research Groups and Their Priorities  
- **EUREC - European Renewable Energy Research Centre**  
   - Funding Opportunities: H2020 projects focusing on renewable technology advancements.  
 
- **European Energy Research Alliance (EERA)**  
   - Research Goals: Integration of renewable energy into power systems, flexibility solutions, collaborative projects.  

## Funding Opportunities  
- **Horizon Europe**: Aiming to strengthen the EU’s research and innovation capacities in the green energy transition.  
- **Interreg Europe**: Funding cooperation on cross-border learning in energy and innovation.  
  
## Conclusion  
The European Union is positioning itself as a global leader in the transition to flexible, renewable energy systems. Future research will increasingly focus on optimization techniques and integration strategies to enhance system performance and reliability.
